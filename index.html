About Continuum ABA

Neurodivergent-Affirming
Care for Real Life Skills

Continuum ABA is a neurodivergent-affirming clinic supporting children, youth, and families using the principles of Applied Behaviour Analysis. We believe every learner has strengths — our role is to build on them.

We specialize in building meaningful, real-life skills that make a real difference — from communication and social connections to emotional regulation. Our approach is collaborative, respectful, and grounded in evidence.

What We Offer

Our Services

Tailored, evidence-based support for learners and families at every stage.

Individual Sessions

One-on-one ABA therapy tailored to your child's unique goals. We work directly with the learner in a supportive, strengths-based environment using naturalistic teaching.

  • Personalized goal setting
  • Progress tracking & reporting
  • Naturalistic teaching

Social Groups

Small-group programs designed to build social connections and practice real-world skills in a safe, fun, and peer-supported environment.

  • Peer interaction practice
  • Structured & naturalistic activities
  • Confidence-building focus

Caregiver Coaching

We partner with families to extend learning into everyday life. Caregivers gain practical tools and strategies to support their child's goals at home and in the community.

  • Home skill generalization
  • Strategy & tool training
  • Ongoing collaborative support

Remote Services

Flexible virtual sessions for families who prefer to connect from home. We deliver the same evidence-based ABA support online, wherever you are in Ontario.

  • Telehealth consultations
  • Virtual caregiver coaching
  • Province-wide accessibility
Ontario Autism Program

AccessOAP Eligible Services

Our services are eligible under the Ontario Autism Program (OAP) via the AccessOAP portal. We work with families to navigate funding options and access services through OAP Core Clinical Services and Caregiver-Mediated Early Years programs.

The Story Behind Continuum

Why Jess Started
Continuum ABA

Some paths feel less like choices and more like a calling you finally stop putting off.

For Jess, that calling has always centred on children — the quiet thrill of watching a child reach a milestone they've been working toward, or seeing a family discover a rhythm that finally feels sustainable. A parent's confidence growing alongside their child's. Those moments don't get old.

What became impossible to ignore, though, was the gap. Families across Lanark County and the surrounding communities were navigating real challenges with too few local supports to lean on. Waitlists grew. Children went without. And the families who needed the most help were often the ones waiting the longest to receive it.

Continuum ABA exists to help close that gap — to bring individualized, evidence-based therapy to more families across the region, and to help grow the local capacity that communities like this one deserve. Because every learner is different, every program here is built from the ground up around the child it serves. That kind of flexibility isn't just possible at Continuum; it's the whole point.

It starts with the kids. It grows with the families.
And it's built to show up — every step of the way.
